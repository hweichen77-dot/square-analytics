## Design Context

### Users
Student interns and business advisors who use the app **at the store** — near the register or in the back office. Sessions are a mix of quick daily check-ins (interns reviewing shifts) and deeper periodic reviews (advisors evaluating performance). The audience is technically capable but the physical setting is bright and sometimes distracting.

### Brand Personality
**Precise, warm, ambitious** — a student-run business that takes its data seriously. Should project credibility and competence to advisors without feeling cold or corporate. This is their store and their data, not a SaaS template.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Warm dark editorial.** Dark mode (suited for store environments and long sessions), but distinctly warm — amber-tinted neutrals instead of cold slate-blue. Electric blue accent for a modern, crisp look. Typography-first layout that makes data feel like information, not decoration.

Anti-references:
- No generic SaaS admin (blue-accent, identical card grids, chart-everywhere layouts)
- No corporate enterprise gray (table-heavy, SAP/Salesforce aesthetic)
- No AI color palette (cyan-on-dark, purple gradients, neon accents)

### Design Principles
1. **Data is the hero** — numbers should be large and legible first; chrome and decoration second
2. **Warm but sharp** — amber tones create ownership without sacrificing precision
3. **Editorial not templated** — asymmetric rhythm, ruled lines over card boxes, no identical grids
4. **Credible under scrutiny** — looks good on a projector or in a presentation to advisors
5. **No AI tells** — every banned pattern (side-stripe borders, gradient text, generic card shadows) is explicitly removed
